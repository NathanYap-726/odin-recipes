Fettuccine with Shrimp Tomatoes and Spinach Cream Sauce



Description

No need to use a lot of kitchen utensils for this dish. A one-skillet pasta that is colourful and fresh-tasting. It goes great with some simple tossed salad
or a great bread. Feel free to substitute for any other pasta of your choosing. This dish takes roughly 40 minutes and provides roughly 4 servings with around
540 calories and 32 grams of protein per serving.


Ingredients

  • 8 ounces fettuccine

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • 4 garlic cloves, divided

  • 1 pound shrimp peeled, deveined, and patted dry

  • salt and freshly ground black pepper, as needed

  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced shallot (~ 1 large shallot)

  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

  • 2 cups halved cherry tomatoes

  • 1/4 cup minced flat-leaf parsley

  • 1/2 cup dry white wine

  • 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped

  • 1 cup heavy cream

  • flat-leaf parsley sprigs for garnish

Directions

  1. Fill large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a boil. Cook the pasta of choice, in this case fettuccine until the tenderness of your choice is reached.
    Reserve about 1/2 cup of pata water and drain the rest.

  2. Heat butter ad olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat until butter is melted. Slice 2 garlic cloves, add to skillet, and cook until lightly browned.
    Roughly 2 minutes, Remove garlic from skillet and discard.

  3. Carefully add shrimp to skillet with a pinch of salt and pepper to follow. Cook until shrimp is pink, opaque, and curled, 2-3 minutes per side.
    Remove from skillet and keep warm.

  4. Add shallot to the skillet and cook, stirring occasionally until softened, 2-3 minutes; stir in red pepper flakes. Mince remaining 2 garlic gloves;
    add to skillet and cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  5. Add sliced tomatoes, minced parsley, and white wine. Bring to a boil, and cook for 2-3 minutes. Stir in chopped spinach; cook until just wilted and bright green.
    Return cooked shrimp to the skillet; stir in cream. Season to taste with salt and pepper.

  6. Remove from the heat. If sauce is too thick, stir in 1 tablespoon pasta water at a time, until desired consistency is achieved. Stir in pasta and ladle into
    serving bowls. Garnish with additional flat leaf parsley sprigs.
