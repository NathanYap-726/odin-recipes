Creamy Lemon Parmesan Chicken


Description

This lemon Parmesan chicken recipe is an “easy, yet impressive, dish to accomplish,” according to recipe tester Nathan.

A nice chicken with some cheese and lemon. A meal that can be made so quick even a child could do this with a blindfold.

(Please don't actually have a child alone in the kitchen without supervision)

Ingredients

  • Cheese:Of course you'll need cheese. While this recipe does call for parmesan you can use any cheese of you're choice.
    (Unless it's blue cheese, please don't use this. No one will survive that.) You'll also need mascarpone(or cream cheese) for the rich sauce.

  • Flour: Cover the chicken throughly before browning for the perfect crisp.

  • Chicken: If you didn't know chicken was needed for this recipe then I think you have other issues to worry about. Maybe start with instant noodles.
    You'll need 8 boneless skinless chicken thighs which will make approximately 4 servings.

  • Seasonings: If you don't want your chicken tasting like water then you'll need seasonings. Season the chicken with salt and black pepper.

  • Oil: Brown the chicken in olive oil for best results.

  • Garlic: Three cloves to add that extra kick to your meal.

  • Broth: Use store or homemade chicken broth. Can never have enough chicken flavor

  • Lemon: Again if you didn't know this recipe requires lemons then you might want to start with somthing simpler. This recipe will use the entire lemon,
    from the zest to the juice.

  • Cornstarch: Make a slurry with the cornstarch and add to the mixutre to thicken the sauce later.

  • Herbs: Used to garnish the dish at the end for a nice pop of colour.

How to make the Lemon Parmesan Chicken

  1. Mix 1/4 cup of Parmesan with flour and season the chicken.

  2. Cover the chicken in the flour mixture.

  3. Brown the chicken on all sides and remove from skillet. After all the chicken is browned use the oil still in pan to cook the garlic.

  4. Mix the broth, mascarpone, lemon zest and juice, and remaining parmesan.

  5. Add the mixture to the skillet and bring to a boil.

  6. Make a cornstarch slurry and whisk into the broth mixture

  7. Add the chicken back into the mixutre and cook until no longer pink. Depending on the thickness of the chicken thigh take extra precaution
    in making sure it is throughly cooked. You could also cut the chikcen into pieces before putting into mixture to make it cook faster.

  8. Plate and garnish with the herbs of your choosing and place a lemon wedge on the side.
